Preparations are beginning for track.easterbunny.cc Version 7!

Posted: June 28, 2023
Share this articleShare this article: https://track.easterbunny.cc/news/#pid-144

It's with great pleasure that I announce the 5th and final major version of track.easterbunny.cc - Version 7 for 2024.

Version 7 of the track.easterbunny.cc brings some nice-to-have features into the tracker to further enhance the tracking experience. It represents the final vision of track.easterbunny.cc and the Easter Bunny Tracker that I've always wanted to make since 2019.

Without further ado, let's get into the planned changes for this year!

Multi-metrics support - finally!
On both mobile and desktop devices, you'll finally be able to view multiple metrics at once. This means you can easily keep an eye on all your favorite metrics without flipping between them!

This feature will be activated through settings by changing the metric visible setting into checkboxes from radio buttons. From there, you can select as many metrics as you want to be visible.

Baskets prepared metric during pre-tracking For our final year of pre-tracking, we're going out with a bang making the experience more interactive. Throughout pre-tracking, you'll be able to see the number of baskets prepared & loaded as the Easter Bunny's helpers get everything ready for liftoff.

You'll be able to see these metrics on both desktop and mobile devices.

Spoken pronunciation in the stop information window
Have you ever been exploring the map and come across a stop that you've wanted to know how it's pronounced? With track.easterbunny.cc Version 7, you'll now easily be able to listen to the pronunciation of a city name!

At the top of the stop information window, you'll find a speaker icon that allows you to play the pronunciation for each city on both mobile and desktop devices.

Right now we're planning for all voices to be in US English and TTS generated, however, depending on the complexity of this feature we may be able to get voices in UK, Australian, and Canadian English and have it automatically switch depending on your device's locale.

The Easter Bunny goes to space in the final release!
A long time ago, we had planned for the Easter Bunny to show a stop on the ISS, but unfortunately this feature never manifested. Nonetheless, for the final TEBCC release we'll be adding this feature! More information about it will come in a developer update, but this is your confirmation that it'll happen!

Unconfirmed features
For Version 7, there's two unconfirmed features at this time. They may get implemented depending on time constraints and the difficulty of them development-wise.

First, we're thinking of potentially adding some new metrics this year. Our first idea is an overall progress metric so you can get a birds-eye view of how the Easter Bunny's journey is going so far.

Second, we're thinking about improving map movements especially in large cities. This would depict the Easter Bunny slowing down when reaching/leaving large cities, on top of the current arrival system that we have.

More information and updates on the status of these features will come at a later time!

Other small features & improvements
With every TEBCC release there'll be some smaller improvements to refine the tracking experience. In no particular order for this year:

  • Improving reliability of the tracker compared to the v6 run (hopefully no more white screens!)
  • Slightly improved stop detection compared to last year.
  • New feature to enable stop arrival times when hovering over baskets on the map
  • New feature for streamers to disable the distance from you metric in the cycle of metrics so it cannot accidentally be shown on streams.
  • Updating the FAQ pages to accurately reflect the shutdown of the tracker.
I hope you're excited for these new features to come in Version 7 of track.easterbunny.cc. Development will be getting underway soon!

Tracking for the final time gets underway on Saturday, March 30, 2024 at 2 AM EDT.

An announcement on the future of track.easterbuny.cc

Posted: May 28, 2023
Share this articleShare this article: https://track.easterbunny.cc/news/#pid-141

This is a post I always knew I would have to write at some point, and today is that day.

Back in 2019 when I started making track.easterbunny.cc, I had no clue what it would turn into today. TEBCC has ballooned into a project that has far exceeded my expectations of what it could do, and honestly, what I thought I could even make.

From a very basic tracker with just a few thousand visitors in 2019, to one of the most complex trackers on the internet five runs later getting tens of thousands of hits year after year - it's truly been an honor to make and build this tracker that's received around half a million hits during its lifetime. I have learned so much from running this tracker, but in the process, been honored by the outpouring of support, enthusiasm, and impact the tracker has had on so many people across the world. Thank you to everyone who visited TEBCC at some point between 2019 and now, it really means the world to me.

Nonetheless, running and managing TEBCC is a time and financial burden, especially as a solo developer. Especially as I move into a new phase of life, I cannot make the guarantee that I will be able to run the tracker at the same level that I do now.

I contemplated my options for running the tracker into the future - paths including lowering costs, turning off Twitter updates, and leaving the tracker in a state of maintenance without new features, all of which would lower the operational and financial overheads that the tracker has.

However, doing such goes against why I made track.easterbunny.cc in the first place - to create one of the best Easter Bunny tracker experiences on the internet. It feels wrong to leave the tracker to die a slow death, eventually to be succeeded by another website and for me to pull the plug somewhere down the road.

With this in mind, I have made the decision that track.easterbunny.cc will no longer run after Easter 2024. Easter 2024 will be the 6th and final run of track.easterbuny.cc.

Shutting down the tracker in 2024 was what I determined to be in the tracker's best interests and within reason of what I can commit to for the tracker's future. I want this amazing tracker to end on a high note, not a low note of a slow death. I know that I can commit to providing the best tracking experience for one more year, and that's exactly what's gonna happen.

So, where does this leave the tracker?

For 2024, Version 6.2 will be released which focuses on some improvements I wanted to make but didn't have time to do in the React refactor. It'll be the final vision of what I wanted TEBCC to be, and I hope you're excited to see what changes come along. There's no set list of features right now, this will be determined and announced at a later date.

As of right now, messages have been put up on the tracker indicating this shutdown at the moment. Through the summer, I'll comprehensively update the entire tracker to reflect that our final run will be in 2024.

The 2024 run of the tracker will be like any other run in the last 5 years. However, once tracking is completed and the map screen resets after a few days, there will no longer be a countdown, rather, a message indicating the tracker has shut down.

I know this news will come as a shock - especially with the knowledge that there is no alternate tracker out there with a similarly complex tracking experience. But in life, the greatest things always come to an end at some point. We shouldn't take them for granted - this tracker included. But once the tracker shuts down, I hope you can look back at some of the fond memories of using this website.

I'm still working to get the tracker open-sourced for v6 and v6.2 will be no different. The legacy of TEBCC will live on through the codebase, allowing anyone who's technically inclined to fire up their own tracker and build on top of what I've made. This is why I open-sourced TEBCC, because I wanted to make sure the innovations and inventions I've made can live on once the tracker itself is gone.

Thank you once again for supporting TEBCC. I'm looking forward to the 2024 run of the tracker, so look forward to more information about v6.2 coming soon!

All the best,
Owen

track.easterbunny.cc upgraded to Version 6.1

Posted: March 3, 2023
Share this articleShare this article: https://track.easterbunny.cc/news/#pid-117

Hello everyone! Today I'm happy to announce that track.easterbunny.cc has been upgraded to Version 6.1 with lots of exciting changes. These changes (and bug fixes as always) will help make the tracker the best that it can be. Let's jump into the changes!

New localization tab in settings
Version 6.1 contains lots of changes that relate to localizing the tracker to your specific liking. As such, we've introduced a sixth tab in settings that helps you localize the trackers settings to your liking. As a result of this change, the tracker units setting has moved over to this tab, and can no longer be found in the settings tab. But, let's get into what's in this tab!

Support for changing time units in the tracker
Browsers are notoriously bad at detecting which clock style your device is in. As such, we've now introduced the ability to manually change whether times in the tracker are presented in 12 or 24-hour formats! Similar to the tracker units setting, there's an automatic selection by default, which defaults to the preferred clock style of your locale. There's two manual settings that come after that, 12-hour and 24-hour. These changes apply to the times listed in the stop information modal, and when hovering over basket icons on desktop devices.

Precise location support!
This has been a feature that I've long wanted to introduce and it's finally here! We've always used an approximate way of determining where you are to figure out when the Easter Bunny arrives and how far the Easter Bunny is from you using your IP address. We did this because otherwise the tracker would have to request your location on load, and that's a little suspicious to do! Most of the time, this estimate is decently accurate and pinpoints your general whereabouts. However, we've also known on mobile data that sometimes the location is wrong, and for some ISPs your estimated location just isn't quite right.

That's where precise location comes in. In the localization tab, you can now tell the tracker to query your current location for arrival estimates and a precise distance from you metric. Doing so also unlocks some added benefits in the tracker! Namely, the estimated arrival time will become loads more accurate, updating in quarter hour intervals throughout tracking. When the Easter Bunny is around the corner, you'll get estimates at 45, 30, 20, 10 and less than 10 minutes before arrival. And when the Easter Bunny is nearby, you'll be able to see down-to-the-stop when the Easter Bunny arrives, up to 10 stops before arrival (of course this is your warning to get tucked in bed so the Easter Bunny can deliver your baskets!). With this update, if you're using approximate location, the distance from you metric now updates in 10 mile or kilometer intervals, and you'll need to enable precise location to unlock its full accuracy.

As always, there's a couple notes for this update. Precise location doesn't require a GPS-capable device, you'll be able to use it on Wi-Fi devices as well. Additionally, the tracker won't try to get your precise location after you query it, so it's up to you to click on Precise location again to update your location if you're on the move. Lastly, to conserve resources (as we need to make reverse geocode calls to provide feedback that we found your location correctly), you'll be limited to updating your precise location at most every 30 minutes.

track.easterbunny.cc is the world's most simple, yet advanced Easter Bunny tracker. Our tracker is simple to use and understand, and we have a wide variety of features and systems so that you can customize your tracking experience if you so desire.

We have 20 tips for you on how to get the most out of your tracking experience, similar to what we did last year. Without further ado, here are the tips for this year!

1 - No refreshing is required!
No refreshing is required. Of course it isn't! You don't need to have auto-refreshing plugins to use our site.


2 - This year, we have only one redirect from countdown to pre-tracking
Last year, our tracker had two redirects, one from countdown to pre-tracking, and pre-tracking to tracking. This year, we only have one redirect from countdown to pre-tracking.

During the 2 AM redirect, please be patient as the tracker loads in, as we asked you last year. We don't expect any issues and for everyone to have the tracker loaded within 30 seconds. If the tracker doesn't load within 30 seconds, refresh the page.


3 - Change settings before tracking starts so the tracker is configured
You can change tracker settings before tracking starts. Settings will carry over from the countdown page to the tracker. We recommend for the best experience that you pre-configure the tracker so you don't have to mess with it once tracking begins.


4 - Settings are saved automatically every time they're changed
As you change settings, you'll see a message pop up saying "Settings have been saved". As you go along changing settings, they will change the tracker in real time. You'll also be able to see some changes, like the appearance and map mode change in the background as you are changing them.


5 - Want to change the default zoom level? Use URL arguments
We default the tracker zoom level to 5 on mobile devices and 6 on desktop devices. If you're not okay with this, you can use the URL arguments feature to set the default zoom level when the tracker loads, and when the map is recentered. This is known as a full zoom override.

If you want your default zoom level to be 8, for instance, you can request the tracker by using this URL: https://track.easterbunny.cc/?zl=8

If you only want to change the zoom on load, and not change the zoom level for when the map is recentered, you can instead use a partial zoom override by appending ! to the end of the zoom level: https://track.easterbunny.cc/?zl=8!

You can use the URL arguments feature to change many other aspects of the tracker. Check out the docs here: https://track.easterbunny.cc/urlarguments


6 - Looking for the track.easterbunny.cc Version 3 experience? The tracker is configurable to the v3 experience!
We planned to implement a reset button for the classic default settings that the tracker ran with in 2019, but didn't have enough time.

For the 2019 look, set the appearance to dark, map mode to hybrid, and make sure the bunny bouncing effect is also off. For the true 2019 look, you can also turn off the estimated arrival time (but it's a cool feature so we recommend you leave it on)


7 - You'll want to turn off the Easter Bunny arrival time estimate if you're streaming, or if you have a smaller device
We allow you to turn off the Easter Bunny arrival time estimate (which is new this year) in settings. This is good if you don't want to reveal your location when streaming the tracker.

This also causes issues on smaller phones (usually on 5 inch and under phones) where the text will hide the Easter Bunny icon. If you don't want this, then we recommend turning off the feature on smaller phones.


8 - Using a VPN will cause inaccurate arrival time estimates.
We use an IP-based system to determine your approximate location. If you have a VPN on, we'll estimate the location at the VPN server, rather than your location.

To get around this, turn off your VPN before the tracker transitions from countdown to pre-tracking/tracking, then turn it back on. If at any time you need to refresh the tracker, follow the similar procedure - turn off your VPN, load the site, then turn it back on.


9 - If you're travelling (which you shouldn't be), refresh your page every so often so that the arrival time can update
When the tracker loads, we calculate your arrival time once for the remainder of your session on the website. If you are travelling (which you shouldn't be given the current situation with COVID-19), refresh the tracker every hour or two so that the arrival time is more accurate.


10 - Settings are applicable to one browser on one device. If you're using multiple devices for tracking, set the settings on each device to the same!
We do not have, and don't expect to implement any sort of cloud saving for your settings. Right now, all settings are stored on your device, and are specific for one browser on your device.

If you use multiple browsers, say Chrome and Firefox, your settings will not sync between the two. Chrome will have its own settings, and so will Firefox.

The same goes if you're using the tracker on multiple devices. Settings will not sync as we do not have any cloud saving functionality. To keep things consistent, you'll probably want to set the same settings for all of your devices.


11 - The Easter Bunny bouncing effect will use more battery, turn this off if you're trying to save battery!
The Easter Bunny bouncing effect doubles the CPU usage of the tracker (from about 6% to 12% on desktops in testing). This also happens on mobile devices, and will mean less battery life on mobile devices.

If you're using the tracker and need to conserve battery life, make sure this option is off.


12 - Need to zoom in on the tracker page? Use Street mode with Light mode forced on
As was the case last year, you'll still see gray horizontal lines on the map when the page is zoomed in, mostly due to issues with the Google Maps API that we use. This effect can be minimized when the tracker is in light mode with street mode enabled.


13 - If you're recording a timelapse of the tracker, refresh the tracker every 6-8 hours
We've noted an issue where the bunny will start to become more jittery after about 10 hours of continuous usage in timelapses. Refresh the tracker every 6-8 hours to get the best timelapse possible. The tracker loads in less than 5 seconds, so you should be able to sneak in a tracker load at the right time.

Given that this is our first year of operations, you might not know how our tracker will work. That's okay, as we've compiled a quick list of tips to get the most out of your tracking experience.

1. No refreshing is required, no auto-refresh plugins are needed

You probably know this by now, but our tracker doesn't require any refreshing. Think of our tracker as the NORAD of Easter Bunny Tracking, you don't need auto-refreshing plugins to constantly update the site.

2. You'll be automatically redirected from the countdown to pre-tracking, then to tracking

Similar to how NORAD runs their tracker, you'll be automatically redirected every step of the way, no matter what device you're on. If you manage to leave your laptop or phone closed on the countdown, and then turn it on when tracking is active, you'll get properly redirected. No links to click, no nothing!

3. You can still change your zoom level when the map is centered on the Easter Bunny

As the title for this tip says, you can zoom in/out as much as you'd like an stay centered on the bunny, even if your zoom in/out gestures get a bit messy. Just remember - don't zoom in too far, or the map will constantly be loading in satellite imagery and you'll have a poor experience.

4. You can set the view of the tracker back to default by clicking twice on the Easter Bunny

Uncentering, then recentering the Easter Bunny will set the tracker to the proper zoom level and positioning, if you want to return to the default map view of the tracker.

5. Need to zoom in on the whole tracker page? Use Firefox or Edge.

There are known issues with Chrome where zooming in on the entire tracker page will cause annoying gray lines between map tiles. Use Firefox or Edge if you want to zoom in on the tracker page, but don't want a degraded tracking experience.

6. Set your bookmarks to track.easterbunny.cc for the best possible loading times during tracking

I know that this may be tricky given that the tracker will bring you to the countdown page, but if you can, bookmark the tracker, but modify the URL to just https://track.easterbunny.cc. It'll get you to the tracking super fast when it starts.

7. Have a little patience when you are redirecting to pre-tracking and tracking

Although we're doing our best to ensure that the redirection process should go smoothly, we can't confidently say that the site won't have any issues come 2 AM & 5 AM EDT on April 20, when the pre-tracking & tracking redirections occur. Please be patient when these times roll around, and wait about 10-20 seconds for pre-tracking/tracking to load. If it takes longer, manually refresh the page.

Just have some fun and track the Easter Bunny! That's what we're here for, and will always be about.
